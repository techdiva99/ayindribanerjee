The Journey to a Consumer Healthcare Company: Why AI-Powered Efficiency Starts With Empathy, Not Algorithms
The healthcare industry is racing to adopt AI — but most organizations are building for payers, not patients. This talk traces the journey from payer-first legacy systems to patient-first AI-powered efficiency, revealing why organizational design, governance, and human empathy matter more than the model itself. Drawn from 15+ years building AI across regional plans and large healthcare companies.
45-min Keynote
60-min Workshop
2
I Built Great Models. Then I Watched Them Die. Here's What I Built Instead.
A practitioner's story of building wound detection algorithms, clinician matching systems, and risk assessment models — and discovering that none of them could survive without the right organizational foundation. Covers the 7-layer AI readiness stack, why governance is how patients learn to trust AI, and how to design the adoption pathways that let AI reach the bedside.
30-min Talk
45-min Keynote

Architecture Degree to AI Strategy: The Case for Non-Linear Thinkers in Healthcare
From designing buildings in Kolkata to leading AI strategy at a large healthcare company in Boston — a career story about cognitive diversity, the power of outsider thinking, and why the $250M algorithm nobody expected came from someone whose resume "didn't make sense." A personal talk about reinvention, belonging, and building technology that serves humans first.
